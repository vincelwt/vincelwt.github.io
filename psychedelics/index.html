On Psychedelics

Vince Lwt | 11 Dec 2018

An increasing number of people (mainly millennials) are getting interested in psychedelic drug usage.
Consequently, more and more actions and awareness are raised in this space, which is awesome.

Personally, I'm eager to see what more improvements psychedelics will have on people / the world.

They temporarily change the way we think, can cure addictions and show us that we are nothing - and everything.

Who knows if humans are ever supposed to do this?

Although there is arguably some magic to their usages, it's a mistake thinking they'd solve all your problems magically.
We need to be very careful with these explorations.

Emphasizing education not only on the consumption but everything that comes before and after.

People should be prepared. Of course Set & Setting is important. Having some clue as to how you think, how you think. Having practised meditation will be hugely beneficial during an experience. Having already tried mind-altering drugs will de-stigmatize what's happening to you.

Curiosity, although a very valid reason in my opinion, shouldn't be the only drive to try psychedelics.

A lot of people will find themselves lost, broken or unable to make sense of what's happening during their journey. This is even more intense after a bad trip.

It's really important at this point not to feel alone.
Brain material, video content, literature, thoughts processes and hearing tales of experienced "travelers" are extremely helpful during an experience and during the recovery period.

You might feel like trying to put everything behind. Never talking or thinking about it again. But the mind doesn't work that way.
