Traveling the world with a 20L backpack

by Vince

2019-03-15

This year I decided to go travel the world, with the following trip:

France -> Thailand -> Indonesia -> Philippines -> Japan -> Hawaii -> Canada -> France

I'm actually writing this article on my flight to Jakarta.

I've always been attracted to minimalism and try to keep my material stuff at a minimum. For this trip, I decided to go full in and leave with only an everyday backpack.

People I meet don't believe that bag is the only thing I have for the whole trip and not just a small conveniency bag :)

Pros

  • Save so much time at airports, no need to checkin luggage
  • Can put the whole bag under the seat in planes/trains
  • Can take motorbike taxis conveniently
  • So much more convenient to move
  • Can run with it if needed (yes)

Cons

  • Can't buy useless shit

The backpack

For the backpack I settled on a Mission Workshop 20L Sanction. It's quite expensive, (~$280) but it find it worth.

The top expands (+- 5L). It's made of high quality materials you know won't break on you. It's waterproof so there's absolutely no worries with Asia rains. Â Because it's so compact, it doubles as my day bag once I settle somewhere.

And the most useful to me, even overpacked as hell it feels nice in your back and doesn't hurt.

The downside is its missing more pockets, and the pockets are a bit hard to access when the bag is overpacked.

Packing the bag

You'll want clothes that can dry rapidly. Then you can wash them in sinks and they'll be ready the day after. If you travel in Asia, it'll be very humid so cotton clothes will take forever to dry (AC does help a bit). Also the occasional rain will leave you soaked.

Merino wool and synthetic clothes excel at drying fast. Merino wool clothes are a bit expensive, but so comfortable. You can wear a merino shirt multiple days in a row and it won't stink, assuming you give it some air every night. Bonus, even humid, they don't feel uncomfortable.

Unfortunately before leaving I bought only 1 Merino shirt, which I wear 70% of the time.

I've also got those 2 lightweight compression bags for clothes. One for underwears and one for the rest. Put your clothes in it, and with a zip it compresses them a bit. Makes packing much faster and my stuff stays organised.

I still find some stuff useless. Like the small pharmacy (thanks, mom) : you can buy everything locally. I almost never wear socks so 2 Merino pairs would've been enough. I don't need that extra shirt.

The towel takes a lot of space and 100% of places I've stayed at provide towels. It's only useful once in a while to chill at the beach or in cold places when you don't want to stay wet.
