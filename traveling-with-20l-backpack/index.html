Traveling the world with a 20L backpack

Vince | 15 Mar 2019

This year I decided to go travel the world, with the following trip:

France -> Thailand -> Indonesia -> Philippines -> Japan -> Hawaii -> Canada -> France

I'm actually writing this article on my flight to Jakarta.

I've always been attracted to minimalism and try to keep my material stuff at a minimum even tho sometimes I still buy dump shit. For this trip, I decided to go full in and leave with only my everyday backpack. People I meet don't believe that little bag is the only thing I have for the whole trip and not just a small carry on for the laptop ^^

Pros

  • Save so much time at airports, no need to checkin luggage
  • Can put the whole bag under the seat in planes/trains
  • Can take taxi motorbikes conveniently
  • So much more convenient to move
  • Can run with it (yes)

Cons

  • Can't buy more shit

The backpack

For the backpack I settled on a Mission Workshop 20L Rambler. It's quite expensive, (250€+) but it find it worth.

The top expands a bit (+- 5L). It's made of high quality materials you know won't break on you. It's waterproof so you're never worried with Asia rains.  Because it's so compact, it doubles as my day bag once I settled somewhere.

And the most useful to me, even when it's over packed like hell it feels nice in your back and doesn't hurt.

The only downside is it's missing more pockets, and the pockets are hard to access when the bag is overpacked.

Bag content

  • 1x Icebreaker merino shirt, 1x Lin shirt
  • 1x chemise (for mosquitoes at night)
  • 1x skirt
  • 1x pants
  • 2x boardshorts
  • 1x Uniqlo ULD doudoune (for chilly nights
  • 1x Nike rain-proof / wind-proof vest
  • 1x Merino Buff (Life-changer as a sleep-mask)
  • Socks: 2x cotton , 1x merino
  • Underwear: 2x cotton , 2x Uniqlo synthetic
  • 1x Nike running shoes
  • 1x Towel
  • 1x Inflattable sleep neck
  • 1x Tongues
  • 1x sunglasses
  • 1x Bose QC-35 headset (the best for flights)
  • 1x 2018 Macbook Air
  • 1x Kindle Voyage
  • 1x Travel power adapter
  • 1x Aukey Quickcharge USB C
  • 1x Small utility bag (putting passport, usb C adapter, papers, etc..)
  • 1x Card wallet
  • Pharmacy (Anti mosquito, after bite, smecta)
  • Toilet stuff: razor, toothbrush, toothpaste
  • Essential oils: Pepper mint for waking up and Jojoba for skin hydration

I've also got those 2 lightweight compression bags that I use for clothes. One for underwears and one for the rest. Put your clothes in it, and with a zip it compresses them a bit. Makes packing much easier and faster, and let stuff organized.

You might think this is the strict minimum I could get to. Actually no, I still find some stuff useless. For example the small pharmacy (thanks, mom) is useless because you can buy everything in shops. I almost never wear socks so 2 Merino pairs would've been enough. Synthetic underwears are so easy to wash and fast to dry I could've bringed only two. I don't need that extra shirt.

The towel I bringed takes a lot of space, and 100% of places I've sleeped at provide towels. It's only useful once in a while to chill at the beach or in cold places where you don't want to stay wet.
